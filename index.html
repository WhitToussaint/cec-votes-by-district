This map depicts the amount of votes cast for Community Education Councils 1-32 by School District. The data was sourced from the DOE and is linked here. The linked DOE website also includes voting results for CCELL, CCSE, and District 75. Click each district to see the number of votes cast there.

Community Education Councils or CECs are charged with promoting student achievement, advising and commenting on educational policies, and providing input to the Chancellor and the Panel for Education Policy (PEP).

Each school district has their own CEC with 11 members. 9 of the 11 members are elected by parents and guardians with children in the school district and the other 2 members are appointed by their respective Borough President.

Every parent or guardian is provided 3 votes per child in grades K - 8 in each school district.

According to a Chalkbeat article linked here, roughly 22,000 parents across the city or just 2% of those eligible, voted in the CEC elections.
